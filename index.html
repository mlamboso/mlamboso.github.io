20/20 Vision

Having 20/20 vision does not necessarily mean you have perfect vision. 20/20 vision only indicates the sharpness or clarity of vision at a distance.

A graduate, but not really.



Quantum mechanics explain how things behave on a very small scale which is nothing like how waves and particles behave. They do not behave like clouds, or billiard balls, or weights on springs, or like anything you’ve tried in the science laboratories. Because atomic behavior is so unlike ordinary experience, both novice and expert physicists fail to fully understand it the way they would like to. However, in a lot of ways, it is only perfectly reasonable that they should not understand it because all human experience and intuition directly applies to only large objects. The “big world” is what we know and understand. Our human conscience and intuition drives what we could and should do. How very uncommon it is to think about subatomic details as we, humans, are often consumed enough by things more directly impactful to us. These are events we celebrate, or cry for, or maybe sometimes both.

These first few months of the year 2020 was not at all what any of us expected. Just as with every new year that blooms, we can’t help but be hopeful. It is, after all, the prime of a new decade. It was supposed to be a promising year for us, new graduates and young professionals. We were supposed to kick-start a career. But here we are, graduates but not really.

A month before the city’s lockdown, I was a scrambling graduating senior, attending to my duties as Editor-in-Chief of the La Salle Yearbook, finishing our design project, taking my finals, and managing the pressure of a Latin Honor nominee. It was tiring, for sure, but I was determined to finish. And I did. We finished our design project and were given the chance to share our design and advocacy to numerous schools. I took my finals and with every pen stroke, I imagined graduation day. Fulfilling my academic obligations meant I can maximize my time to transition the yearbook organization to the succeeding batch and so, we opened applications. The team and I were already very excited with the arrival of the yearbooks almost two weeks before graduation. We gushed over the yearbooks and immediately planned for the release on graduation day. Everyone who knows what goes on in the stage of pre-press will agree with me when I say how challenging it is to get everything done completely and on time. Of course, we were excited for everyone to see it. But of all the little tasks I assigned every staff for graduation day, preparing to shut off the office was not on our list. We had just finished interviewing new applicants when news broke out that the campus, nay the city, will be shutdown. It was when I last stepped into the office – my little home in campus for two years.

The words I wrote for the yearbook publication couldn’t be more accurate. “The experiences we had in the past years with this University will become our anchor as we cross to the unpredictable future.” And boy, how unpredictable is the world today? I once had a planner called “Design your Life.” Besides the section for daily planner, there were also pages reserved for writing yearly goals, recognizing fears, determining monthly milestones, tracking savings and expenses. I bet no planner can help me design my life now. I can only hope to stay determined and focused. This is what “20/20 Vision” to be all about. It gives a call to maintain a clear view in the midst of the chaos – to stay focused.

To the graduates of Class 2020, if there were words more commendable than the simple congratulations, I’d use it. Good wishes! Best regards! Felicitations! No matter how fancy the word I choose to use, the reply would be the same: thank you. Because what else will you say when you are congratulated? It is simply what you feel at the moment – simply thankful.

Although graduation is a pride taken from us, it doesn’t make our hard work invalid.

Although graduation is a pride taken from us, it doesn’t defeat how much we learned.

Although graduation is a pride taken from us, it doesn’t weaken our hearts.

To the graduates of Class 2020, keep your vision at 20/20.
